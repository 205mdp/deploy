E:1.6 SHELTER-IN-PLACE

Depending upon the nature of the emergency, it may be safer to keep guests and associates inside the Hotel rather than evacuate. Such events that require shelter-in-place include:

Weather emergencies

Chemical release outside Hotel

Natural Gas leak outside

Terrorist threats or actual release of weapons of mass destruction

(Modified shelter-in-place procedures may be required for such events as blackouts around the hotel or civil disturbances outside which would require guests to be protected inside the Hotel.)

OPERATOR

  • At the direction of Director of Security or the General Manager, make a specific announcement requesting all guests and associates to remain inside the hotel. The specific content of the message will be provided.

DIRECTOR OF SECURITY

  • Quickly close all exterior doors and windows.
  • Post security personnel or designated associates at all Hotel exits/entrances to ensure guests and associates remain inside and that exterior doors remain closed.
  • Select interior rooms above the ground floor with the fewest windows or vents to temporarily house guests and associates not required to assist with shelter-in-place procedures. The room or rooms should have adequate space for everyone to be able to sit. Avoid overcrowding as it may create discomfort and panic.
  • Access to hard-line phone is preferred as cellular service could be overwhelmed by a widespread event.
  • Have access to television to monitor media reports.
  • If there appears to be a danger of explosion outside, close all window shades, blinds or curtains.
  • Seal any air vents into the selected rooms.
  • Keep all entrances to selected rooms closed as much as possible and seal areas around doorways with towels, tablecloths or other malleable material.
  • Gather essential emergency supplies such as non-perishable food, bottled water, battery-powered radio, batteries and first-aid kit.
  • Gather information on all guests and associates present and review status and assist any guests with special needs such as the disabled.
  • Monitor public media and law enforcement to determine when emergency has cleared. Do not attempt to determine by personal exploration whether threat has ended.

ENGINEERING

  • Turn off all HVAC equipment to prevent spread of dangerous airborne agents.
  • Using duct tape and plastic sheeting or bags, cover all accessible air inlets which draw outside air into the HVAC system.
  • Assist security in sealing room or rooms selected for shelter-in-place.

FOOD & BEVERAGE

  • If possible without adding personal risk, gather bottled water or other sealed beverages which do not need refrigeration, along with non-perishable food items.
  • Ensure all kitchen heat sources are turned off.
