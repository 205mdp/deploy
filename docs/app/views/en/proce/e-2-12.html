E:2.12 HOSTAGE SITUATION

Hostage incidents create a unique set of challenges. Because lives are in immediate danger, it is likely that both the hostage and the hostage taker will be highly emotional and perhaps irrational. As a result, it is important that all hotel associates remain calm and give the appearance of control and calmness at all times.

Any associate who becomes aware of a hostage situation should immediately contact Hotel Security. DO NOT attempt on your own to rescue the hostage or overpower the hostage taker; that is best left to trained law enforcement personnel.

SECURITY

  • Notify the Emergency Response Team and ask them to contact Security Control immediately. (Note: DO NOT announce over the radio that there is a hostage situation).
  • Contact local emergency services (9-1-1 in the U.S.); give them all the details that are currently known.
  • Calmly evacuate the area and cordon off the surroundings.
  • Notify the Director of Security.
  • Assist police in establishing emergency phone lines if requested to do so.
  • Assist the General Manager with the above tasks, or any task deemed necessary.
  • At the conclusion of the hostage incident, prepare a full report.

DIRECTOR OF SECURITY OR ON-DUTY DESIGNEE

  • DO NOT attempt to interfere with law enforcement officials or endanger yourself or others in any way.
  • Obtain as many details about the incident as possible. If the hostage taker is making demands, relay the information to police.
  • If it has not already been done, quickly and calmly evacuate the immediate and surrounding areas. Cordon off the affected area to prevent unauthorized people from entering.
  • Provide police with floor plans and information regarding utilities.
  • Assist in establishing emergency phone lines if requested by police.
  • Make provisions to establish a Command Post for the hotel, and a separate one for police, if needed.
  • Make provisions for the control of utilities (e.g. shutting off power and water), if directed by police.
  • Establish a de-briefing room, and at the conclusion of the incident, conduct a de-briefing session as soon as possible.

GENERAL MANAGER

  • Contact the Division Representative to the Corporate Crisis Response Team
