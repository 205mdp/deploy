E:2.13.2 ASSOCIATE ACCIDENT OR INJURY

Any associate injured at the Hotel should contact Hotel Security immediately.

If the injured associate is not conscious or able to contact Human Resources or Security, the nearest associate should contact Hotel Security. Do not provide assistance unless you are certified to practice First Aid or CPR. Stay with the injured associate until help arrives.

Accidents that result in medical treatment beyond First Aid or associates’ lost paid time should be handled according to Workers’ Compensation procedures.

ASSOCIATE

  • Report all injuries. Complete an Associate Accident Report. Accident report must be neatly printed or typed and distributed to the Department Head or Supervisor and Security immediately.
  • If the injured associate leaves the Hotel in order to receive professional medical care, he or she must report to Security or Human Resources before returning to work. This is to ensure that the associate has a medical professional’s permission to return to work.
  • If additional medical care is needed after initial treatment, notify Human Resources and the Security Director or Assistant Director.

DEPARTMENT HEAD OR SUPERVISOR

  • If Security has not already been contacted, contact them immediately for all injuries. Relay the location, identify the associate and the extent of injury. If the injury is minor, the associate can be escorted to Security Control.
  • Determine the extent of the injury.
  • If necessary, request the assistance of personnel trained in first aid or Emergency Medical Response.
  • Complete an Associate Accident Report. Accident Reports must be neatly printed or typed and distributed to:
    • Safety and Security Department (Original document)
    • Department Head
    • General Manager
    • Human Resources

SECURITY

  • Assess the severity of the injury.
  • If required, contact local emergency services (9-1-1 in the U.S.).
  • Dispatch a Security officer to the front entrance to guide arriving paramedics.
  • Contact Rick Management.
  • If the injury involves five or more people or a fatality results, contact the Division Representative to the Corporate Crisis Response Team and notify OSHA within 48 hours.
