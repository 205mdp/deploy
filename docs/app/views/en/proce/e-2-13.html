E:2.13 INJURY OR ILLNESS

E:2.13.1 GUEST MEDICAL EMERGENCY

Handling a medical emergency will depend, in part, on whether the injured or ill person is conscious and able to make their own decisions or not.

First, any associate who becomes aware of a medical emergency should contact Hotel Security and remain with the injured/ill person until help arrives. Associates should not attempt to provide assistance unless they have been trained in CPR.

If the individual is not conscious or able to make decisions on treatment, then local emergency services (9-1-1 in the U.S.) should be called.

If an individual is conscious and apparently capable of making decisions, the individual should make a choice on what service they require, where they would like to be taken and by whom they are to be treated.

Provide a list of several hospitals, clinics or doctors, and if warranted offer to call emergency services.

SECURITY

  • When receiving a call regarding a medical emergency, immediately determine:
    • The location of the emergency?
    • The type of injury or other nature of the emergency?
    • Is an ambulance required?
    • Other medical attention required?
    • Is the person breathing?
    • Does the person have a pulse?
    • Is the person conscious?
    • What is their approximate age?
    • Is there a known medical history?
  • Notify Security & Emergency Response Team of a medical emergency and location.
  • Be prepared to contact local emergency services (9-1-1 in the U.S.).
  • Obtain first-aid kit/supplies.
  • Respond to the scene to ensure safety. Provide first-aid or CPR as required. Ensure that Bloodborne Pathogen guidelines are being adhered to while administering first-aid and during the clean up of the area in which body fluids may be present.
  • Refer to instructions for Security Director.
  • Provide assistance as needed.
  • Prepare the appropriate incident report with photos.

DIRECTOR OF SECURITY OR ON-DUTY DESIGNEE

  • Respond to the scene to ensure safety. Provide first-aid or CPR as required and if certified in First-Aid and CPR. Ensure that Bloodborne Pathogen guidelines are being adhered to while administering first-aid and during the clean up of the area in which body fluids may be present.
  • Determine what level of medical attention may be required: local emergency services or a private service.
  • If person is not conscious or able to assist in determination of care, contact local emergency services (9-1-1 in the U.S.).
  • If person is conscious and able to assist in determining care needed, provide choice of local hospitals or clinics, unless person specifically requests a facility.
  • Advise the Hotel Security if local emergency services or a private service is needed.
  • If such response is needed, request that the main entrance be cleared of vehicles.
  • Dispatch a member of the Emergency Response Team to meet the responding medical units, and direct them to the scene.
  • If no emergency service vehicles are required, arrange alternate transportation such as a taxi or car service.
  • Advise guest/patron that they will be contacted by the Hotel.
  • Ensure there is follow-up in a timely manner.
  • If guest/patron has been hospitalized, double-lock the room, and arrange for storage of their belongings if long term. Establish a “message” account.
  • If the injury/illness is of a serious nature (life-threatening, loss of limb, etc.) and occurs when senior management is not on property, contact the General Manager, Property Operations Director or appropriate senior management executive at home.
  • Following a serious injury, ensure that Emergency Response Team members (or other associates involved) are gathered for a de-briefing session.

ANY HOTEL PERSONNEL INVOLVED

  • Show concern and compassion to the injured/ill person. BE SINCERE.
  • DO NOT move the injured/ill person except in a life-threatening situation.
  • DO NOT admit fault or negligence.
  • DO NOT assume that the Hotel is liable.
  • DO NOT refer to previous incidents.
  • DO NOT give out copies of Hotel reports.

GENERAL MANAGER

  • Contact the Division Representative to the Corporate Crisis Response Team with specifics of the incident if injury/illness is serious. Otherwise, contact Risk Management.
  • Ensure appropriate follow-up with the injured/ill person.

PAYING FOR GUEST’S MEDICAL EXPENSES

  • Direct requests for Hotel payment of guest medical expenses to one of the following for approval:
    • Security Manager, Director or Assistant Director
    • General Manager
    • Director of Rooms
  • Contingent upon the request’s approval, contact the medical facility and advise them to direct bill to the attention of the approving manager or director at the Hotel.
  • Note: The Hotel’s option to pay for medical expenses is not an admission of liability. Further, those amounts should be reimbursable through general liability insurance.
