E:2.16 OTHER EMERGENCIES

When any emergency occurs, even those that may not be specifically addressed in the manual, the following general guidelines should be followed. If possible, refer to instructions for an emergency that is close in nature to the event experienced.

Notify the General Manager and Security immediately. If necessary, contact local emergency services (9-1-1 in the U.S.). In general, injured or unconscious persons should not be moved unless there is a continuing risk to them if left in place. Remain calm and reassure guests.

GENERAL MANAGER

  • Request all necessary assistance and contact all appropriate emergency response teams, community officials and associates.
  • Maintain a calm attitude and establish a sense of order and organization.
  • Assist emergency personnel and community officials who arrive on the scene.
  • Make every effort to stay informed of accurate facts as they occur whether information is being supplied by the media, community officials or associates.
  • Ensure guest and associate safety is a priority.
  • Assist guests and associates or designate someone to help.
  • Keep guests and associates informed of important facts and instructions. Be compassionate.
  • Direct Managers to keep track of costs associated with the emergency.
  • Complete an incident report, or if the emergency involved a large amount of Security assistance, instruct Security to complete the report. Include all costs, including labor costs, for insurance purposes.
  • Communicate to the Crisis Team representative if warranted.

DIRECTOR OF SECURITY

  • Establish and maintain order during the emergency. Remain calm.
  • Ensure the safety of guests and associates.
  • Cordon off any areas that pose a risk or danger and make certain only authorized personnel have access to the area.
  • Assign Security Officers to patrol the entire facility to make sure Hotel Management is aware of all related events or guest requiring special assistance.
  • Assist emergency personnel and community officials who arrive on the scene.
  • Answer guest and associate questions with calmly stated, factual information.
  • Assist guests and associates as needed.
  • Regularly report status updates to the General Manager.
  • Ensure that adequate emergency supplies are on hand.
  • Keep track of emergency-related costs, including labor.
  • Complete an incident report if Security was more involved in the emergency than the General Manager.

DEPARTMENT HEADS AND MANAGERS

  • Ensure the safety of your staff members. Account for each staff member that is on-duty during the emergency.
  • Relay important information and instructions to your staff members.
  • Assist the General Manager as needed.
  • Keep track of costs related to the emergency. Report this information to the General Manager or Security.

ENGINEERING

  • Immediately assess whether or not utilities need to be either shut off or restored in affected areas.
  • When necessary, supply building plans to help officials navigate through the building when responding to an emergency.
  • Ensure the safety of guests and associates as a first priority. Secure and prevent property damage as a second priority.
  • Update the General Manager with status information regularly.
