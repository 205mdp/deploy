E:2.17 STRUCTURAL COLLAPSE

Associates should immediately contact local emergency services (9-1-1 in the U.S.) in the event that the Hotel or a part of the facility structurally collapses. Assist with guests or associates who may be injured, but IN GENERAL, DO NOT MOVE INJURED OR UNCONSCIOUS PERSONS unless there is immediate danger of more collapse nearby. Remove everyone else from the area as soon as possible. Remain calm and encourage guests to stay calm.

Help emergency response units as they arrive on the scene. Ensure that all guests and associates are not in any danger as they help rescue workers identify and assist any one who may be trapped. Stay clear of any stray and potentially unstable debris, particularly glass and objects that could fall.

Media inquiries should be directed to the General Manager, who should direct those inquires to the local authorities.

FRONT DESK

  • Print out a list of all guests and associates, particularly any lists that would help rescue workers identify people in the collapsed area. Provide to the Emergency Response Team.
  • Answer guest questions and reassure them that everything is being done to handle the situation. Remain calm.
  • Help keep guests away from unstable debris that could pose additional dangers such as falling objects and broken glass.

GENERAL MANAGER

  • Instruct Security to set-up an Emergency Command Post. Ensure the Command Post is stocked with emergency supplies.
  • Establish and staff a “Missing Persons Reporting Area” where guests and associates can officially report anyone they fear may be in the collapsed area. Regularly submit updates to emergency response teams.
  • Request that guests report back if they discover the person they believed was missing was not harmed in the collapse.
  • As guests and associates are found, update the list.
  • Assign an associate to relay information about specific persons as they are found.
  • Have Managers report to the Emergency Command Post for instructions. Each Manager should report any missing associates who were on-duty during the collapse. Submit these names to emergency response teams.
  • Remind all associates, guests and rescue workers that the area is stable and they should be careful to avoid glass and objects that could fall.
  • Direct Food and Beverage to supply food and water to rescue workers.
  • Assist emergency personnel as they arrive on the scene.
  • Direct Managers to keep track of costs as much as possible during the emergency.
  • Remain calm and encourage associates to stay calm and reassure guests.
  • In the event evacuation is required, follow Evacuation procedures in Section E1.2.
  • Contact the Division Representative to the Crisis Response Team.

OPERATOR

  • If Senior Management and Department Heads are not on the property, begin contacting them at home. Request that they report to the Hotel.
  • Be prepared to assist in contacting outside agencies.
  • Keep phone lines and radio channels open for emergency communication.
  • Reassure guests that the Hotel is doing everything possible to handle the situation.
  • Notify Hotel Central Reservations and request that they handle all reservation requests during the emergency.

DIRECTOR OF SECURITY OR DESIGNEE

  • Establish and staff an Emergency Command Post. Gather emergency supplies such as flash lights, first-aid kits, hard hats and two-way radios and bring them to the Command Post.
  • Obtain a list of guests and associates from the Front Desk.
  • Cordon off the area and ensure that only authorized personnel are allowed to enter.
  • Cordon off or remove additional debris such as glass and fallen objects that could pose additional risks.
  • Be aware as you work around the scene that the area is unstable.
  • Assist emergency response crews as needed.
  • Direct guests and associates with questions about missing persons to the “Missing Persons Reporting Area.”
  • Keep the General Manager regularly informed of status updates.
  • Patrol the area and assist special needs guests or mildly injured persons.
  • Answer guest questions with calmly-stated, factual information as it is available.
  • Complete an incident report after the emergency has been resolved.

ENGINEERING

  • Immediately shut off water, power and gas to the collapsed area of the building.
  • Obtain building plans and submit to emergency response teams.
  • Remove loose debris that could pose additional hazards, such as broken glass and unstable objects that could fall.
  • Assist emergency response crews.
  • Help emergency response crews obtain additional rescue equipment such as cranes and generators, if necessary.
  • Update General Manager with status information regularly.

HOUSEKEEPING

  • Gather all clean blankets and bring them to the Command Post.
  • Help distribute blankets to affected associates and guests.

FOOD AND BEVERAGE

  • Supply bottled water and food to rescue workers and affected guests and associates.
