E:2.21 UTILITY OUTAGES

E:2.21.1 GAS LEAK

If a gas leak is suspected, immediately notify engineering and security as quickly as possible. Extinguish any open flames and do not use fire as a light source or for any other reason. Do not use electronic devices that could generate sparks.

If evacuations are deemed necessary, associate should take appropriate actions to move all guests and staff to a safe area. Assist any guests with special needs. Be prepared to ventilate the affected area if needed.

GENERAL MANAGER

  • Should immediately investigate whether there is a gas leak and instruct engineering to shut off the gas as soon as possible.
  • Instruct associates to extinguish any flames in the area as well as to shut off electronic devices being used.
  • Remove guests from affected area if necessary.
  • Notify the local gas company to immediately investigate the leak and to get the problem fixed quickly.
  • Record the number of rooms and areas affected and log the costs to file a report for insurance purposes.

SECURITY

  • Evacuate the affected area and guard it to ensure all guest and associates are safely away from the area.
  • Assist general manager and engineering to keep everyone safe and maintain order when evacuating an area of the hotel.
  • Assist emergency crews if they are needed.
  • Write a report with photographs if appropriate.

ENGINEERING

  • Turn off gas to affected area or entire hotel if the source of leak is not known.
  • Investigate the source of the leak and determine if it can be repaired quickly.
  • Assist local gas company in their assessment of the situation to fix the leak.
  • Prior to restarting gas supplies, ensure all equipment is manually turned off and inspect for any gas buildup. Turn on kitchen exhaust fans prior to gas activation.
