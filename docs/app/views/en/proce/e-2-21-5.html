Checklist

E:2.21.5 TELECOMMUNICATIONS FAILURE

In the event of a phone system failure, associates should notify the Operator and Front Desk Manager if they are not already aware of the situation. Remain nearby to assist the Operator and Manager.

If guests inquire, let them know that the situation is under control and the Hotel is working to restore communications as quickly as possible. Use cellular phones to make any necessary emergency calls until the phone system is working properly again.

OPERATOR OR FRONT DESK MANAGER

  • Assess the situation and contact the General Manager and IT Manager. Relay all important information.
  • If needed, gather associates and establish a command post.
  • Notify local authorities and the telephone utility service, if necessary, that the phone system is down.
  • Communicate via cellular phone.
  • Assign associate to check the function of all public telephones.

DIRECTOR OF ROOMS

  • If the failure is expected to last more than half an hour, broadcast a message on the Fire Alarm Voice System to inform guests.
  • Have cellular phones available at the reception desk and at the concierge station.
  • Assign associates to distribute 10 minute calling cards to guests for use at nearby pay phones.

SECURITY

  • Ensure adequate staffing to maintain order. Officers should be asked to stay on shift until phone service has been restored.
  • Identify any guests with disabilities that may require assistance.
  • Activate Security cellular phone to communicate with local authorities.
  • Assign officers to conduct patrols and assist guests and associates where needed.

ENGINEERING

  • Ensure that there is adequate staffing by requesting engineers to stay on shift until the phone system is restored.
  • Discover the cause and extent of the failure.
  • Communicate with Director of Rooms via two-way radio and assist where needed.
