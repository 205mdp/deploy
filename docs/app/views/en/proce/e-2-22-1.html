E:2.22 WEATHER EMERGENCIES

E:2.22.1 FLOOD

Flooding caused by rising water from an overflow of a body of water or a severe storm may take place suddenly or with some warning. Experience will identify Hotels most vulnerable to such rising water emergencies. Flooding caused by broken or leaking pipes is covered in Section 2.21.6.

The stages of Flood alerts are as follows. Weather services should be monitored to better prepare.

FLOOD WATCH

A flood watch implies that a flood is a real possibility, but is not imminent.

FLOOD WARNING

A flood warning implies that a flood is imminent or is in progress and that immediate precautions should be taken.

FLASH FLOOD WARNING

A flash flood warning implies that a flash flood is imminent or is in progress and that immediate precautions should be taken. A flash flood is a sudden, violent flood after a heavy rain.

If a flood risk is imminent, complete Pre-flood Checklist (attached to the back of this section). If immediate action needs to be taken, evacuate guests and other associates from the low-lying areas, if possible. Move to higher floors using the stairs, not the elevators.

CAUTION: If a shock hazard exists in the affected area. DO NOT ENTER until Engineering has shut off ALL power to the area.

SECURITY

  • Complete Pre-Flood Checklist at the back of this section.
  • Assess the situation to determine:
    • Where is the location and extent of the flooding?
    • Are there any injuries or dangers to residents or Hotel staff?
    • Are there any other dangers, such as an Electric shock hazard?
    • Is the flooding contained or is it spreading?
    • Can emergency supplies reach the area?
  • Notify the following departments:
    • Emergency Response Team
    • Housekeeping Department
    • Engineering Department
    • Guest Services/Valet
  • Control communications by assuring guests and associates that the situation has been reported and response crews are in route.
  • Coordinate and assist with distributing emergency supplies if necessary.
  • Cordon off area until there is no danger
  • Prepare an incident report with photos of damage
  • Refer to instructions for the General Manager and assist as needed.

DIRECTOR OF SECURITY

  • Respond to the scene and assess the situation by determining the cause of the flood and current dangers.
  • Take immediate action to prevent further damage to the property( i.e., request wet vacuums, mops, buckets and necessary clean-up equipment).
  • Call an outside water damage team, if necessary, for water extraction.
  • Cordon off affected areas with “wet floor” signs and relocate guests.
  • Check all adjacent and lower floors for water damage.
  • Advise all departments to closely track all costs associated with the damage, including labor expenses, particularly if water damage appears to exceed the property insurance deductible.
  • NOTE: Property loss exceeding the Hotel property insurance deductible should be reported to the corporate risk management department (or the owner’s insurance company, if applicable) as soon as possible. ENGINEER
  • Assist General Manager as needed.
  • Be prepared to immediately shut-off electrical power to the affected area(s).
  • Attempt to identify the source and take appropriate action to abate flooding if possible.

GENERAL MANAGER

  • Interface with local emergency management authorities for guidance on whether to shelter guests in place or evacuate, and if evacuation is recommended determine location.
  • If there are injuries or fatalities, or significant damage to the Hotel, contact the Division Representative to the Crisis Response Team.
