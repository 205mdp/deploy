E:2.5 CRIMINAL ACTIVITY

Associates should take care to not put themselves at risk if discovering criminal activity. Contact Security and aid victims as described in this section and as appropriate, but do not put yourself at risk in attempting to prevent the activity or in apprehending the perpetrator.

For any criminal activity, complete the Description Form in E2.5.5.

E:2.5.1 ASSAULT, PHYSICAL OR SEXUAL

Any associate who witnesses a physical or sexual assault in progress, discovers an assault victim, or who are themselves the victim of an assault on hotel property should immediately contact Security. Associates should never put themselves at risk.

If the injuries received in the assault are serious or life-threatening, immediately contact local emergency services (9-1-1 in the U.S.) Do not move the victim, but stay with them until help arrives. If the injuries received are relatively minor and immediate emergency medical help is not required, take the victim to a safe and secure spot – such as the Director of Security’s office – immediately contact local law enforcement and attempt to provide aid and comfort until police arrive and/or medical assistance is arranged. In particular, extreme care should be taken with victims of sexual assault, who may be emotionally distraught. In any event, always show concern and compassion toward the victim.

If the victim is female, have a female accompany the response team, whenever possible. Determine as soon as possible if the alleged attacker is believed to be either a guest or associate of the hotel. Provide all information and assistance to law enforcement.

DO NOT comment to guests about an assault that occurs on the property or provide any details about the victim. DO NOT refer to previous incidents that may have occurred at the hotel. If asked specifically by guests about an incident, politely respond that it is being investigated by Security or local law enforcement and comment no further.

SECURITY

  • When receiving a call regarding an assault, immediately determine:
    • The location of the incident
    • If the assault is still in progress.
    • Immediately contact local emergency services (9-1-1 in the U.S.)
    • The condition of the victim. Be prepared to contact local emergency services (9-1-1 in the U.S.)
  • Notify the Emergency Response Team and proceed to the scene. Do not announce over the radio the nature of the situation.
  • Obtain a first-aid kit/supplies and bring to the scene.
  • Keep on-lookers away in a calm manner.
  • Take the victim to a secure location and attend to their physical and emotional needs. Ask if they would like to have anyone contacted.
  • Ensure that police have been notified, if emergency services are not required.
  • See instructions for the Director of Security (below) and assist as needed.
  • If the assault occurred in a public area of the property, determine if videotape of the incident exists. If so, preserve and secure the tape so it is not reused or taped over or otherwise destroyed.
  • Prepare the appropriate incident report with photos or diagrams.

DIRECTOR OF SECURITY

  • Respond to the scene. Cordon off the area and post Security personnel to ensure that only authorized persons are allowed to enter the scene.
  • DO NOT TOUCH OR ALLOW ANYONE TO DISTURB ANYTHING AT THE SCENE.
  • If local emergency services (9-1-1 in the U.S.) has been contacted, dispatch a member of the Emergency Response Team to meet police and fire department personnel responding to the call.
  • If the victim is stable, ask if they know the identity of their attacker. If the alleged attacker is believed to be a guest or hotel associate, immediately begin searching the property and seal off hotel entrance and exit points.
  • If the victim is able to assist in determining medical care needed, provide a choice of local hospitals or clinics, unless the person specifically requests a facility.
  • Determine if there are witnesses to the incident. If so, request that they remain on the property for interview.
  • Obtain as much information as possible for authorities:
    • Identity of the victim and alleged attacker, if known
    • Location and time of assault
    • Witness list
    • Videotape of assault, if available
  • If the victim is a registered guest, have the Front Office print out a folio and obtain a copy of the registration card for authorities.
  • If the guest is hospitalized, double-lock the room and arrange for storage of their belongings if long term. Establish a “message” account.
  • If the assault occurred in a guest room, double-lock the room until police release it.
  • Do not allow the area to be cleaned until police release it.
  • Pull room lock information from the time the guest was registered.
  • If clean up of bodily fluids is necessary, ensure that Bloodborne Pathogen guidelines are followed.
  • Assist local authorities as needed.
  • Notify senior management.
  • Confirm with authorities who will notify family and associates of the victim.

GENERAL MANAGER

  • Refer media to law enforcement for information and comment.
  • Contact the Division Representative to the Corporate Crisis Team with specifics of the incident.
  • Ensure appropriate follow-up with the assault victim.
