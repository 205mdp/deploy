E:2.5.2 HOMICIDE

In the event of a homicide on Hotel property, all associates should keep several fundamental ideas in mind:

  • Do not touch or allow anyone to disturb anything at the crime scene.
  • Do not alarm guests. Respond and react to reports of a homicide in a calm and controlled manner.
  • Isolate area and clear of any guests or unneeded associates.
  • Shield the victim and scene from view of guests.

Any associate discovering an apparent homicide of a guest, visitor or fellow associate should immediately notify Security. Stay in the immediate area until Security arrives on the scene to ensure that nothing is disturbed and to keep on-lookers away.

If unsure whether the victim is dead, contact an associate on duty who is CPR trained.

Though understandably difficult, it is important that all hotel staff keep a professional appearance and attitude so that guests do not panic if and when news of the homicide begins to spread.

Arrange for the nearest available guest room for use by authorities as their command center as the investigation is likely to take quite some time.

Rumors may quickly begin to spread among guests – even among associates. If questioned by others who have heard rumors or witnessed some activity, associates should simply say there is a medical emergency or something similar. Let them know that the matter is being handled, but more information is not available at the moment. DO NOT speculate or offer any other comments.

If associates are directly exposed to the action or scene, ensure associates are provided EAP services.

Media interest in a homicide is likely. However, DO NOT attempt to respond to media inquiries. Instead refer all inquiries to the General Manager. The General Manager should refer all media to the authorities. Associates should be reminded that all media inquiries should be referred to the General Manager.

SECURITY

  • When notified of an apparent homicide on the property, immediately determine:
    • The location of the deceased
    • Whether local emergency services (9-1-1 in the U.S.) has been contacted. If not, immediately do so
    • If there are any witnesses
    • If anyone in the area is acting suspicious
  • Notify the Emergency Response Team. Do not announce over the radio the nature of the situation.
  • Respond to the scene. ENSURE THAT NOTHING IS DISTURBED.
  • Keep on-lookers away in a calm manner.
  • Use back-of-the-house elevators if at all possible.
  • If the homicide occurred in a public area of the property, determine if videotape of the crime exists. If so, secure the videotape.
  • See instructions for the Director of Security (below) and assist as needed.
  • Assist authorities if needed.
  • Prepare the appropriate incident report with photos and diagrams.

DIRECTOR OF SECURITY

  • Respond to the scene. Cordon off the area and post Security personnel to ensure that only authorized persons are allowed to enter the scene.
  • DO NOT TOUCH OR ALLOW ANYONE TO DISTURB ANYTHING AT THE SCENE.
  • Keep a log of all persons entering the scene, including times.
  • Dispatch a member of the Emergency Response Team to meet police responding to the call.
  • Obtain as much information as possible for authorities:
    • Who discovered the body
    • Time of discovery
    • Name and address of deceased, as well as persons knowing the deceased
    • Witness list
    • Any reports of suspicious activity
    • Videotape of the crime, if available
  • If the deceased was a registered guest, have the Front Office print out a folio and obtain a copy of the registration card for authorities.
  • Pull room lock information for the entire period the guest was registered and give to authorities.
  • If the homicide occurred in guest room, double lock the room until police release the room. (Note: Inventory of the deceased’s property shall be done by police and documented by Security).
  • Do not allow the room to be cleaned until it is released by law enforcement.
  • If clean up of bodily fluids is necessary, ensure that Bloodborne Pathogen guidelines are followed.
  • Assist local authorities as needed.
  • Notify senior management.
  • Confirm with authorities that they will notify next of kin.

GENERAL MANAGER

  • Refer media inquires to law enforcement.
  • Contact the Division Representative to the Corporate Crisis Response Team.

HUMAN RESOURCES DIRECTOR

  • Contact EAP to deploy counseling resources as needed.
  • Advise associates of EAP and counseling services available.
