E:2.5.4 THEFT

Theft is considered the undetected stealing of a guest’s items (for example, money, jewelry, luggage) from a guest’s room/car or the hotel’s public areas, as opposed to a physical robbery in which an assailant confronts a guest in a threatening manner to steal items.

If any guest, visitor or associate reports a theft, immediately contact Security. Stay with the guest until Security arrives and always exhibit genuine concern about their loss. DO NOT comment to the guest or visitor about any previous incidents that may have occurred on the property.

A victim of theft may be irate, stunned or even fearful upon discovering their loss. They may blame the hotel and its security (or perceived lack thereof) for the theft of their items. It is important, therefore, that all associates maintain a calm and professional demeanor when dealing with a victim of theft so that the guest is made to feel more secure and comfortable, and an unpleasant situation does not escalate further.

SECURITY

  • When notified of a theft, immediately respond to the scene. ENSURE THAT NOTHING IN THE THEFT AREA IS DISTURBED.
  • Talk with the victim and get as much information as possible, including a description and estimated value of the item(s) stolen.
  • Determine if there are any witnesses to the theft.
  • Notify police of the crime, providing as much detail as possible.
  • Keep any on-lookers away in a calm, professional manner.
  • Determine if videotape of the theft exists.
  • See instructions for the Director of Security (below) as assist as needed.
  • Prepare the appropriate incident report with photos or diagrams.

DIRECTOR OF SECURITY

  • Respond to the scene. Cordon off the area if necessary.
  • Interview the victim. Determine the victim’s recent activities to try and establish a time frame for the theft.
  • Ask victim and associates if they noticed any suspicious persons or activity in the area.
  • Interview witnesses, if available.
  • Turn over all information to police and assist as needed.
  • If the victim is a registered guest, have the Front Office print out a folio and obtain a copy of the registration card for authorities.
  • If the theft occurred from a guest’s room, pull room lock information for the entire period the guest has been registered and give to authorities.
  • If theft occurred from a guest room, double lock the room until police release it for clean up.
  • Notify senior management.

GENERAL MANAGER

  • Follow-up with victim and express sincere concern.
  • Contact the Division Representative for any significant theft.
  • Advise appropriate Risk Management or insurance representative.
