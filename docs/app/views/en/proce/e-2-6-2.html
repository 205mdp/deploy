E:2.6.2 DROWNING

When a member of hotel staff discovers a guest or associate who has apparently drowned – in the hotel pool, at the hotel’s private beach, or even in the bathtub – the first response should be to immediately remove the person from the water.

Contact Hotel Security or call the emergency services number (9-1-1 in the U.S.).

Attempt at once to determine if the person has a pulse or is breathing. If so, begin providing CPR, if certified. If not certified in CPR, remain on the scene until help arrives.

If the person is obviously deceased, while awaiting Security attempt to keep on-lookers away from the scene. DO NOT speculate or comment to any on-lookers about how or why the drowning may have occurred.

SECURITY

  • When receiving a call regarding a possible or confirmed drowning, immediately determine:
    • The location of the incident
    • If the person has a pulse or is breathing
    • If the person is conscious
    • Whether local emergency services (9-1-1 in the U.S.) has been contacted. If not, immediately do so.
  • If a drowning is confirmed, notify the Emergency Response Team. Do not announce over the radio the nature of the situation.
  • Respond to the scene. Keep on-lookers away in a calm manner.
  • See instructions for the Director of Security (below) and assist as needed.
  • Prepare the appropriate incident report with photos or diagrams.

DIRECTOR OF SECURITY

  • Respond to the scene. Cordon off the area and post Security personnel to ensure that only authorized persons are allowed to enter the scene.
  • DO NOT TOUCH OR ALLOW ANYONE TO DISTURB ANYTHING AT THE SCENE.
  • Dispatch a member of the Emergency Response Team to meet police and fire department personnel responding to the call.
  • Obtain as much information as possible for authorities:
    • Who discovered the body
    • Time of discovery
    • Name and address of deceased, as well as persons knowing the deceased
  • If the deceased was a registered guest, have the Front Office print out a folio and obtain a copy of the registration card for authorities.
  • If the deceased drowned in a pool or beach staffed with a lifeguard, determine the whereabouts and actions of the lifeguard at the time of the drowning and immediately afterward.
  • If the deceased drowned in the bathtub, pull room lock information for the entire period the guest was registered and give to authorities.
  • Once the deceased has been removed from the property, double-lock the victim’s room until police release it for clean up. (Note: Inventory of the deceased’s property shall be done by police and documented by Security).
  • Do not reopen the pool or beach until authorized to do so by police. If clean up of bodily fluids is necessary, ensure that Bloodborne Pathogen guidelines are adhered to.
  • Assist local authorities as needed.
  • Notify senior management.
  • Confirm with authorities that they will notify next of kin.

GENERAL MANAGER

  • Refer media inquires to law enforcement.
  • Contact the Division Representative to the Corporate Crisis Response Team.
