E:2.6.3 NATURAL DEATH

Natural death is defined as death that occurs without apparent reason, such as due to disease or injury or foul play.

Hotel staff should react quickly and discreetly if they discover an associate, guest or visitor who has apparently passed away from natural causes. Any person finding what they believe is a deceased person should immediately contact Security. DO NOT attempt to move the body.

Care should be taken not to alarm any guests. If questioned by on-lookers who have witnessed activity surrounding the deceased, associates should merely say there is a medical emergency or something similar. Offer no details and do not confirm there is a death. If the person inquiring is aware of the death do not speculate as to the cause of death.

SECURITY

  • When receiving a call regarding a death by apparent natural causes, immediately determine:
    • The location of the deceased
    • If the person has been checked for a pulse or breathing
    • Whether local emergency services (9-1-1 in the U.S.) has been contacted. If not, immediately do so
  • If death is confirmed, notify the Emergency Response Team. Do not announce over the radio the nature of the situation.
  • Respond to the scene. Keep on-lookers away in a calm manner.
  • See instructions for the Director of Security (below) and assist as needed.
  • Prepare the appropriate incident report with photos or diagrams.

DIRECTOR OF SECURITY

  • Respond to the scene. Cordon off the area and post Security personnel to ensure that only authorized persons are allowed to enter the scene.
  • DO NOT TOUCH OR ALLOW ANYONE TO DISTURB ANYTHING AT THE SCENE.
  • Dispatch a member of the Emergency Response Team to meet police and fire department personnel responding to the call.
  • Obtain as much information as possible for authorities:
    • Who discovered the body
    • Time of discovery
    • Name and address of deceased, as well as persons knowing the deceased
  • If the deceased was a registered guest, have the Front Office print out a folio and obtain a copy of the registration card for authorities.
  • Pull room lock information for the entire period the guest was registered and give to authorities.
  • Once the deceased has been removed, double-lock the room until police release the room for clean up. (Note: Inventory of the deceasedâ€™s property shall be done by police and documented by Security).
  • If clean up of body fluids is necessary, ensure that Bloodborne Pathogen guidelines are followed.
  • Assist local authorities as needed.
  • Notify senior management
  • Confirm with authorities that they will notify next of kin.

GENERAL MANAGER

  • Refer media inquires to law enforcement.
  • Contact the Division Representative to the Corporate Crisis Response Team.
